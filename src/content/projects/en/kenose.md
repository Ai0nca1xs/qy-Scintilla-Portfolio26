This ongoing project (Kénose / Kenosis) will be the central axis of my diploma
year.

The initial goal was to design a world that invites slowing down. I first
considered object design, but identified a fundamental contradiction: any
material design that optimises life inevitably increases global entropy. The
limit of materials is a problem of scale, not of technology. My approach thus
shifted from the object to the construction of an entire universe.

Currently at the stage of texts and sketches, I present here its basic
architecture.

The universe of KÉNOSE is set in a hyper-technological future where humanity
enjoys infinite life. Death is no longer an end but a ritual of active choice
— the only experience through which the weight of life can be felt.

KÉNOSE is an underground space devoted to this ritual. The individual enters a
module shaped like a three-dimensional Star of Bethlehem. Body and
consciousness separate; consciousness directs the funeral. At the centre lies
an apple — a micro black hole condensing the data and memories of an entire
life. As it is tasted, consciousness retraces everything and the flesh
dissolves. The former life becomes a seed, grows, bears fruit and takes human
form again, re-entering the world as a new immortal entity.
